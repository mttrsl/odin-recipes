Smash Burgers

Description

Smash burgers are a popular style of burger known for their thin, crispy-edged patties, achieved by pressing ground beef firmly onto a hot griddle or skillet. The smashing process maximizes contact with the cooking surface, creating a flavorful crust through the Maillard reaction. Typically seasoned with just salt and pepper, the focus of a smash burger is on the beef's natural flavor and its caramelized exterior.

Served on a soft bun, often with melted cheese, pickles, and a tangy sauce, smash burgers are prized for their simplicity and satisfying crunch. Their relatively quick cooking time and customizable toppings make them a favorite at diners, food trucks, and home kitchens alike. The technique and presentation celebrate a minimalist approach to burgers while delivering bold, crave-worthy flavors.

Ingredients

  • 125g of raw lean beef mince
  • Garlic granules
  • Salt
  • Black pepper seasoning
  • Brioche bun
  • Slice of lettuce
  • 2 light cheese slices
  • Sauce of choice

Method

  1. Season the raw beef mince with the garlic granules, salt & black pepper seasoning.
  2. Roll into balls and smash into patties using a flat spatula.
  3. Pan fry each side of the patty for 5 minutes on a medium heat
  4. Melt the cheese slices onto the patties, 1 slice per patty.
  5. Stack the patties, then add them to the brioche bun with lettuce and your choice of sauce.
